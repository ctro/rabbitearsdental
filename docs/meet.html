Meet the Team!

Dr. Troy Markgraf

Dr. Troy Markgraf grew up in north Denver and completed his undergraduate studies at the University of Colorado. After graduation, he led a nonprofit supporting underserved youth. Dr. Markgraf’s time with this community exposed him to profound disparities in healthcare access, which ultimately inspired him to pursue a career in dentistry. He earned his DDS from the University of Colorado School of Dental Medicine and has since cared for patients throughout Northern Colorado.

Dr. Markgraf currently lives in Greeley, Colorado with his partner Daniela, and their cat Gilly. His two children Zoe and Maximus, are also proud graduates of the University of Colorado. Outside of dentistry, he enjoys skiing, cycling, playing the mandolin, spending time outdoors, and cheering on the Nuggets, Avalanche, and Broncos.

Moving to the mountains has always been a dream for Dr. Markgraf. He is grateful for the opportunity to live and practice in Steamboat. He looks forward to building meaningful relationships with patients and providing compassionate, conservative dental care to the community he now calls home.

Dr. Joy feels fortunate to welcome a colleague who shares her values of empathy, careful attention to detail, and clear communication. She trusts Dr. Markgraf’s dental experience wholeheartedly and is confident in the quality of care he provides. Dr. Joy extends her sincere gratitude to her patients for their continued trust and support, as the practice grows within our wonderful community. Please join us in welcoming Dr. Markgraf to Rabbit Ears Dental.

Dr. Joy

Dr. Joy Troxel completed her undergraduate studies on a full academic scholarship to Indiana University graduating at the top of her class with Bachelor of Science Degrees in Biology, Chemistry, and Psychology. She continued her graduate education at Indiana University earning her Master of Science Degree in Biology. Dr. Joy was awarded her Doctor of Dental Surgery Degree at the Indiana University School of Dentistry with highest distinction. Dr. Joy was accepted to Harvard University School of Dental Medicine’s Public Health Residency and Master of Public Health Programs. She is a member of the American Dental Association, Society of American Indian Dentists, American Association of Women Dentists, Colorado Dental Association, and the Western Colorado Dental Society. Dr. Joy practiced dentistry for several years in Indiana before moving to Colorado with her husband, Alex. In her free time she enjoys all things outdoors; ultramarathon trail running, nordic skiing, paddling whitewater, biathlon, and camping with her dog, Olive.
